# Moodie demo video draft

Status: Published at [YouTube](https://youtu.be/OOV0_9EMOUk). Final duration: 2:24.

## 0:00 to 0:20

Show the Moodie landing page and the moving hero.

Voiceover:

> No two people feel the same. Moodie is social film discovery built around that belief. Instead of treating someone as ratings or a point in a recommendation system, it records what a film meant to them, finds people who felt something familiar, and shows what moved those people next.

## 0:20 to 0:40

Show the old January baseline briefly, then return to the current product.

Voiceover:

> The idea began years ago as a student project called EmotionFlix. It centered facial recognition and never became the product I wanted. I left it untouched. OpenAI Build Week and the capabilities I saw in Codex with GPT-5.6 Sol pushed me to reopen it and rebuild it as Moodie.

## 0:40 to 1:15

Choose `Enter demo`. Show a complete response in the feed, open the diary, and open the response composer.

Voiceover:

> A response starts with a film, your own words, and feelings you choose directly. You can keep it private or publish it. An optional photo can add personality, but Moodie never analyzes it and never lets media decide what you felt. The diary keeps every viewing, including rewatches, as its own record.

## 1:15 to 1:45

Show the recommendation path from a connected person and shared film to a new film. Use the Whiplash, Ananya, and Scream path if it is easy to reach during recording.

Voiceover:

> Recommendations come through people. Moodie compares feelings on films two people have both recorded, then finds another film backed by that person's real public response. This path can recommend Scream for joy through Ananya because she experienced it joyfully and we already found common ground on Whiplash. It is not an emotion-to-genre rule. It is a human path.

## 1:45 to 2:20

Show the repository, a short commit view, the seeded feed with varied writing and photos, and the local `.agents` skill.

Voiceover:

> I used Codex as a working partner across the repository. It untangled immature frontend and backend code, ran the product, tested the recommendation engine before integration, simplified the runtime, generated palette studies and candid response images inside coding sessions, and built a deterministic social demo with 12 people and 163 responses. As it learned my corrections, it reviewed the sessions and created a local Moodie skill so the product's design history could stay with the repository.

## 2:20 to 2:45

Show the response composer, people page, and one final recommendation explanation.

Voiceover:

> GPT-5.6 Sol made the largest product correction. It recognized that facial recognition was not the real idea and pushed the emotional record back to the person. That changed the recommendation engine, the data model, and the interface. I kept steering the result toward something personal, human, clean, and simple. Codex made that process direct enough to finish during Build Week.

## 2:45 to 2:55

Return to the landing hero or a strong people-led recommendation screen.

Voiceover:

> Moodie is what the original idea was trying to become. No two people feel the same, and that should be the reason discovery starts with people.
